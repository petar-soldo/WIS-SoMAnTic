The SoMAnTic Project

The Social Media Analysis Toolbox (SoMAnTic) was developed as part of the Web Information Systems course within the Master of Digital Humanities at KU Leuven.

The Problem

Social media platforms have become central to public discourse, cultural exchange, and political mobilization. However, accessing and analyzing data from Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) has become increasingly difficult for researchers. Due to strict data protection measures (such as the DSA) and closed APIs, researchers are often forced to work in highly restrictive Virtual Computing Environments, limiting the tools they can use and preventing them from combining datasets easily.

Our Solution

SoMAnTic aims to bridge this gap. Rather than acting as a data scraper, our platform is designed to provide an accessible interface for analyzing already retrieved datasets. We built this tool specifically for researchers in the humanities and social sciences who may not have extensive programming backgrounds.

By offering an interactive web interface, SoMAnTic allows users to easily navigate and analyze complex data, featuring tools for demographic breakdowns, sentiment analysis, and linguistic tracking.
